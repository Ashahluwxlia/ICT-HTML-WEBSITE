Technology Choices

Types of X-ray Imaging

Radiography:

The most common form of X-ray. It is used for imaging broken bones, teeth, and the chest using low radiation.

Fluoroscopy:

In this X-ray type, the radiologist can observe the patient's X-ray in real time and take pictures. It uses more X-ray radiation than a standard X-ray but still at low levels.

Computed Tomography (CT):

The patient lies on a table and moves through a scanner during a CT scan. A fan-shaped X-ray beam passes through the patient onto detectors. The patient slowly enters the machine to create a 3D image composed of many "slices." CT scans use a higher dose of X-rays because multiple images are obtained in one session.
